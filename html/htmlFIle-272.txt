<script type="text/javascript">
  var navDropdown = (function() {

    function init() {
      calculateDropdownWidth();
      bindEvents();
    }

    function bindEvents() {
      $(window).resize(function() {
        if ($(this).width() > 991) {
          calculateDropdownWidth();
        }
      });
    }

    function calculateDropdownWidth() {
      //Calculate left dropdowns
      var leftMaxOffset = $('.header__nav--left > ul').offset().left - 20;

      $('.header__nav--left .has-dropdown').each(function(i, el) {
        var $el = $(el);
        var count = $('ul li', $el).length;

        var linkW = $('> a', $el).outerWidth();
        var menuW = (count*133) + (count*20) + (10*2);
        var menuLeft = (menuW - linkW) / 2;

        if ($el.offset().left-menuLeft < leftMaxOffset) {
          menuLeft =  $el.offset().left - leftMaxOffset;
        }

        $('ul', $el).css({
          width: menuW + 'px',
          left: -menuLeft + 'px'
        });
      });

      //Calculate right dropdowns
      var rightMaxOffset = (
        Math.ceil($('.header__nav--right > ul').offset().left) 
        + Math.ceil($('.header__nav--right > ul').outerWidth())
      );

      $('.header__nav--right .has-dropdown').each(function(i, el) {
        var $el = $(el);
        var count = $('ul li', $el).length;

        var linkW = $('> a', $el).outerWidth();
        var linkRight = linkW + $('> a', $el).offset().left;
        var menuW = (count*133) + (count*20) + (10*2);
        var menuRight = (menuW - linkW) / 2;

        if (linkRight+menuRight > rightMaxOffset) {
          menuRight =  rightMaxOffset - linkRight;
        }

        $('ul', $el).css({
          width: menuW + 'px',
          left: 'auto',
          right: -menuRight + 'px'
        });
      });
    }

    return { 
      init: init, 
      calculateDropdownWidth: calculateDropdownWidth 
    };
  })();

  $(function() {
    navDropdown.init();
  });
</script>

				<div ><div id="faq"><div class="small-on-md-up"><div class="small-on-md-up"><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: Are you pet friendly?</strong></p><div><p>A: Regrettably we do not allow pets on the farm. We have resident tortoises, ducks, chickens and children on the farm, and we would not want to potentially harm them. Guide dogs welcome.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: Do you take bookings for The Greenhouse?</strong></p><div><p>A: At The Greenhouse we allocate tables on a first come, first served basis between 10h00 and 16h00. Please arrive early to avoid a long queue during busy times. For groups of between 13 and 18 guests, please do make a reservation.&nbsp;For groups of more than 18 people, contact <a href="mailto:functions@babylonstoren.com" onmousedown="dataLayer.push({'event': 'eventTracker', 'eventCat': 'Email Clicks', 'eventAct': 'Click', 'eventLbl': 'functions@babylonstoren.com', 'eventVal': 0});">functions@babylonstoren.com</a> for our private venue rates and packages.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: Do you have a play area for kids?</strong></p><div><p>A: We offer a farm and garden experience; children love the animals and space outside.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: Are you wheelchair friendly?</strong></p><div><p>A: Babylonstoren is a farm and garden experience engaging all the senses. The nature and extent of the garden with uneven paths of gravel, pips and sticks make&nbsp;it very challenging for wheelchairs. We do not have staff available to escort guests but offer wheelchairs at the farm shop for those with strong family members or friends able to assist.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: How old is the garden?</strong></p><div><p>A:&nbsp;The farm dates back to 1692. The oldest buildings were erected in the late 17th century, with the manor house dating back to 1777. After the property was bought in 2007, French-Italian architect Patrice Taravella, owner of <a href="http://www.prieuredorsan.com/" onmousedown="dataLayer.push({'event': 'eventTracker', 'eventCat': 'Outbound Links', 'eventAct': 'Click', 'eventLbl': 'www.prieuredorsan.com/', 'eventVal': 0});">Prieur&eacute; Notre Dame d&rsquo;Orsan</a>, designed the garden. Over the next few years pergolas, gravel pathways and water canals were built. During 2009 the gardeners started preparing the soil and did most of the planting for the formal garden. Babylonstoren opened its doors to the public in November 2010.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: What do you farm &quot;outside&nbsp;the garden&quot;?</strong></p><div><p>A: We grow plums and citrus for export. For our own use we have olives, grapes, figs, prickly pears, persimmons and now also blood oranges, specially for our garden juices. In addition we grow mealies, rye, wheat, alfalfa and risotto rice. We alternate the rice with waterblommetjies &ndash; after the rice harvest, waterblommetjies are planted in the same dams. Our almond and pecan trees do not produce nuts yet but are growing beautifully and before long, we&rsquo;ll have our first harvest. We make our own rooibos and honey bush teas. We also grow proteas and make our own compost for use in the garden and on the farm. A big camp is being built at the moment. This is where we&rsquo;ll house our own free-range pigs.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: How do you pronounce Babylonstoren?</strong></p><div><p>A: Listen to the pronunciation&nbsp;<a href="javascript:pronouncebbs()">here</a>.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: Where does the name come from?</strong></p><div><p>A:&nbsp;Babylonstoren&rsquo;s life started in 1692, when Governor Simon van der Stel granted the free burgher Pieter van der Byl a small piece of land at the foot of a conical hill. It was dubbed, in Dutch, Babilonische Tooren, later Babilonstoring or Babylonstoren, as the 17th-century farmers thought its shape resembled the Tower of Babel mentioned in the Bible. An interesting but unproven speculation is that it also suggests the linguistic melting pot the valley became at that time, with Dutch, French, German and various Khoi and San languages intermingling, and exotic Asian words sailing in with the spice trade &ndash; all of which evoke the biblical story of the Tower of Babel.</p></div><hr class="margin-bottom-25px" /></div><div style="position:relative"><p><strong>Q: What does the logo represent?</strong></p><div><p>A:&nbsp;The Babylonstoren logo, which consists of the pipe (representing the&nbsp;farmer), the flower (representing the garden) and the bird (representing nature), combines the very essence of Babylonstoren &ndash; keeping things simple and as true to the earth as possible.</p><hr /><p><strong>Q: Why do you ask an entrance fee and what is it used for?</strong></p><p>A:&nbsp;When visitors come to Babylonstoren, whether it&rsquo;s for lunch or a garden tour, an entrance fee is required for access to our 3.5 hectare garden which is the heart of Babylonstoren. On weekends and public holidays entry costs R20 per person (R10 per child under 12), but on weekdays it is R10 for all. Babylonstoren places great value on the upliftment of the community and therefore all entrance fees go to our Babylonstoren Trust. The Trust raises funds for community projects such as the Learning Centre where our farm workers&rsquo; children are cared for while their parents are hard at work in our vineyards and orchards. Meals are provided as well as help with school work. The Trust also provides support with private and other school fees for older children and it funds sporting equipment and clothes as well as transport to and from sports days.&nbsp;</p></div></div></div></div></div></div>
